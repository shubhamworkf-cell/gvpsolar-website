FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions —
Solar Energy in Maharashtra

Everything you need to know before going solar in Maharashtra — answered by our experts.

What is the cost of a 5kW solar system in Maharashtra in 2025?
+
A 5kW on-grid solar system in Maharashtra costs approximately ₹2.80–₹3.20 lakh before subsidy. After claiming the ₹78,000 PM Surya Ghar subsidy, the final cost is ₹2.02–₹2.42 lakh. This system generates 20–25 units/day and saves ₹4,000–₹6,000 per month on your MSEDCL bill.
What is the maximum solar subsidy I can get in Maharashtra in 2025?
+
Under PM Surya Ghar, maximum subsidy is ₹78,000 (for systems 3kW and above). Under the new Maharashtra SMART Scheme, BPL/SC/ST families can get up to 95% subsidy — paying as little as ₹2,500 for a complete solar system. For farmers, PM KUSUM provides up to 90% subsidy on solar irrigation pumps.
Does GVP Solar Energy handle the PM Surya Ghar subsidy application?
+
Yes — completely and free of charge. GVP Solar Energy manages every step: portal registration on pmsuryaghar.gov.in, document upload, MSEDCL coordination, net meter application, inspection follow-up, and subsidy disbursement tracking. You don't need to visit any office. Call +91-7665165666 to start today.
What is the payback period for industrial solar in Maharashtra?
+
Industrial solar in Maharashtra typically has a payback period of 3–5 years depending on capacity and consumption. With the current HT industrial tariff of ₹9.20/unit and solar cost of ₹1.80–₹2.50/unit amortized over 25 years, the net saving per unit is ₹6.70–₹7.40. A 500kW plant can save ₹55–₹70 lakh annually, paying back in ~4–5 years.
What is the Maharashtra SMART Solar Scheme?
+
The Swayampurna Maharashtra Residential Rooftop Solar (SMART) Scheme was launched in October 2025 by the Maharashtra state government. It provides additional state-level subsidies on top of the Central Government PM Surya Ghar subsidy. BPL and SC/ST families in Maharashtra can get combined subsidies of up to 95%, paying as little as ₹2,500 for a complete 1kW solar system.
What solar panels does GVP Solar Energy use?
+
We use only Tier-1 MNRE-approved solar panels — brands include Waaree, Adani Solar, Vikram Solar, and REC. These are globally certified panels with 25-year performance warranties. We never use cheaper, unbranded panels regardless of cost savings. For inverters, we use Sungrow, Growatt, Delta, and SMA — all with proven service networks in Maharashtra.
Is solar worth it for a home in Kolhapur or Ichalkaranji?
+
Absolutely yes. Maharashtra has 250–300 sunny days/year, MSEDCL residential tariffs are ₹8–₹10/unit, and PM Surya Ghar subsidy reduces upfront cost by ₹30,000–₹78,000. A typical 3kW system in Kolhapur costs ₹92,000–₹1.22 lakh after subsidy, saves ₹2,500–₹3,500/month, and pays back in 4–5 years. Over 25 years, total savings are ₹5–₹8 lakh.
How does net metering work in Maharashtra with MSEDCL?
+
Net metering allows you to export surplus solar power to the MSEDCL grid and receive credit on your electricity bill. MSEDCL installs a bidirectional meter that records both units consumed from the grid and units exported. At billing time, exported units are deducted from consumed units. If exports exceed consumption, credits carry forward. GVP Solar handles the complete MSEDCL net meter application, typically approved in 7–15 working days.
